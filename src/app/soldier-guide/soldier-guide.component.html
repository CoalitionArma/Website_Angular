This guide covers how Coalition conducts warfare in-game, not real-world doctrine. You will be expected to know and perform these techniques in real time during sessions. Take it seriously — your squad depends on it.

Section 1

PLATOON STRUCTURE

A Platoon Element (Platoon Leader and Platoon Sergeant / 2IC) commands up to five squads. Each squad is led by a Squad Leader and Medic, with two color fireteams beneath them.

PLATOON ELEMENT

PL (Platoon Leader) · PSgt (Platoon Sergeant / 2IC)

Commands up to 5 squads

Squad Composition

Each squad has a command element and two color fireteams — up to nine troopers total:

FTL · RAT · RAT · RIFLEMAN

Red team holds position while green team flanks

RED TEAM — Base of Fire

The FTL, AR, and AAR combination delivers sustained suppression. The LMG, underbarrelled grenade launcher, and two sets of binoculars allow Red team to cover Green in almost any environment.

GREEN TEAM — Maneuver

Two RATs and a Rifleman provide the squad's anti-armor punch while flanking pinned enemies. Green team advances while Red team keeps enemies suppressed and heads down.

Red Team

Playing AAR

The AAR is the second man behind the LMG. Stay directly with your AR and FTL while moving. You are their eyes and their ammunition carrier.

  • Spot targets and observe bullet impact with binoculars
  • Carry and resupply spare ammunition for the gun
  • Watch the AR's flanks — he will tunnel-vision on targets
Green Team

Playing Rifleman

The Rifleman is the maneuver backbone of Green team. Your flexibility makes you invaluable — support the FTL, cover the RATs while they set up shots, and be the first through the door.

  • Stick with your Green FTL and follow their positioning calls
  • Cover the RATs' flanks while they identify and engage armor
  • Use grenades and suppressive fire to clear buildings and obstacles
Green Team

Playing RAT

You are often the whole platoon's only anti-armor asset. One shot, one launcher. A missed round can get your whole team killed.

  • Always clear your backblast and call your rocket
  • Know your launcher's backblast clearance before firing
  • Coordinate with your partner RAT — two launchers double your coverage

Section 4

RADIOS / COMMUNICATIONS

All in-squad communication uses in-game push-to-talk radios. Each squad is assigned a radio frequency at mission start. You can press "P" to see all squads and their frequencies.

Squad Net

All members of the squad share one frequency. Each FTL carries one short-range AN/PRC-343. The SL carries two radios — one for the squad net and one for the platoon net.

Platoon Net

The Platoon Element (PL + PSgt) and Squad Leaders share one dedicated channel. Both PL and PSgt carry two radios. Strict radio discipline required — regular soldiers do not transmit on this net.

Callsigns

Callsigns identify who is transmitting. Always state your callsign when you key up — never say "me" or "I".

6

Platoon Leader

Always callsign "6" — e.g. "1-6" for 1st Platoon Leader

7

Platoon Sergeant (2IC)

Always callsign "7" — e.g. "1-7" for 1st Platoon Sergeant

1-1

Squad Callsign

Format is Platoon#-Squad# — 1st platoon, 1st squad = "1-1"

1

Short Callsign (Platoon Net)

On your own platoon net, squads can drop the platoon number and use just their squad number — e.g. "1" for 1st squad. Only one platoon is on that net, so there's no ambiguity.

"6, 1-1 moving to phase line alpha, out."

Section 5

MEDICAL

Open your inventory when injured to see all active wounds. Hover any injury to see its status. You start with 2 bandages, 2 tourniquets, and morphine.

⚠ "Stable" ≠ Bandaged

Stable means: no active bleeding + fluids administered + stable heart rate. Bandaged limbs alone are NOT stable. Failure to get proper treatment can lead to unconsciousness and cardiac arrest.

Self-Aid Steps

  1. 1 Kill the threat and get to cover — do not treat yourself while still under fire
  2. 2 Check for bleeding wounds and apply a tourniquet if not in immediate danger
  3. 3 Apply bandages to limbs if in a safe area and remove tourniquet if applied
  4. 4 Request a medic if in so much pain that it affects your vision
  5. 5 If you're out of supplies or need fluids — tell your FTL you need a medic immediately
  6. 6 Once bleeding stops, fluids are in, and all tourniquets are removed — you're safe to continue

Buddy-Aid Steps

  1. 1 Suppress / kill the threat — never expose yourself to recover a buddy while still in contact
  2. 2 Pop smoke to cover your approach if your buddy is unconscious and in the open
  3. 3 Stop the bleeding — apply tourniquets and bandages to keep them alive until the medic arrives
  4. 4 Call the medic through your FTL with location and number of casualties
  5. 5 Pull security — face out and hold your sector while the medic works

Remember

  • Medical personnel are not guaranteed to be on your short-range channel
  • Route all medic requests through your Fireteam Leader
  • Remove tourniquets once a medic has treated you — they stop circulation
  • Don't wait until you're unconscious — inform leadership early
